# Identification of Candidate Genes for qPCR Analysis in *Prochlorococcus*

## Introduction

Quantitative PCR (qPCR) is a powerful technique for measuring changes in gene expression under different environmental conditions. For this exercise, I selected *Prochlorococcus* as the study organism and focused on the transcriptional response to nitrogen limitation.

---

## Organism

*Prochlorococcus*

---

## Experimental Condition

**Nitrogen limitation (nitrogen starvation)**

Nitrogen is one of the major nutrients limiting primary productivity in oligotrophic oceans, where *Prochlorococcus* is the dominant photosynthetic microorganism. Nitrogen limitation has been shown to induce extensive changes in gene expression, including genes involved in nitrogen regulation and assimilation (Tolonen et al., 2006). Therefore, nitrogen starvation is an appropriate experimental condition for investigating transcriptional responses using qPCR.

---

## Target Gene 1 — *ntcA*

**Why did I choose this gene?**

I selected **ntcA** because it is a central regulator of the nitrogen starvation response in cyanobacteria. Tolonen et al. (2006) reported activation of the **NtcA regulon** during nitrogen stress in both *Prochlorococcus* MED4 and MIT9313, indicating its important role in regulating genes involved in nitrogen metabolism.

**Known biological function**

The **ntcA** gene encodes a global transcriptional regulator that controls the expression of genes involved in nitrogen uptake and nitrogen assimilation.

**Expected change in gene expression**

Based on the transcriptomic analysis by Tolonen et al. (2006), **ntcA** expression is expected to increase during nitrogen limitation as part of the regulatory response to nitrogen starvation.

**Why is this gene relevant?**

Because **ntcA** regulates the expression of multiple nitrogen-responsive genes, it is an excellent molecular marker for monitoring the activation of the nitrogen stress response.

---

## Target Gene 2 — *glnA*

**Why did I choose this gene?**

I selected **glnA** because it plays a direct role in nitrogen assimilation. In the transcriptomic analysis by Tolonen et al. (2006), **glnA** was among the genes whose expression increased during nitrogen starvation.

**Known biological function**

The **glnA** gene encodes **glutamine synthetase**, an enzyme that converts ammonium and glutamate into glutamine, a key step in nitrogen assimilation.

**Expected change in gene expression**

Based on the published transcriptomic data, **glnA** expression is expected to increase during nitrogen limitation.

**Why is this gene relevant?**

While **ntcA** regulates the transcriptional response to nitrogen stress, **glnA** represents the metabolic response by directly participating in nitrogen assimilation.

---

## Reference (Housekeeping) Gene — *rnpB*

**Why did I choose this gene?**

I selected **rnpB** because it was used as the reference gene in the qRT-PCR study by Fernández-Pinos et al. (2015) for normalization of gene expression in *Prochlorococcus*.

**Known biological function**

The **rnpB** gene encodes the RNA component of **RNase P**, an essential ribonucleoprotein complex involved in the maturation of precursor transfer RNAs (pre-tRNAs).

**Why do I expect its expression to remain stable?**

Because **rnpB** is involved in an essential housekeeping cellular process and has previously been used successfully as a reference gene in qRT-PCR studies of *Prochlorococcus*, it is expected to maintain relatively stable expression under the experimental conditions, making it suitable for normalization.

---

## Hypothesis

I hypothesize that nitrogen limitation will activate the nitrogen starvation response in *Prochlorococcus*. Based on the transcriptomic analysis reported by Tolonen et al. (2006), **ntcA** expression is expected to increase as part of the regulatory response to nitrogen stress, while **glnA** expression is also expected to increase to support nitrogen assimilation through glutamine synthesis. In contrast, **rnpB** is expected to maintain relatively stable expression and therefore serve as a suitable reference gene for qPCR normalization.

---

## Summary

This qPCR experiment is designed to investigate the molecular response of *Prochlorococcus* to nitrogen limitation.

The selected target genes represent two complementary aspects of the nitrogen starvation response:

- **ntcA** – transcriptional regulation of nitrogen-responsive genes.
- **glnA** – nitrogen assimilation through glutamine synthesis.
- **rnpB** – reference gene for normalization of gene expression.

Together, these genes provide a suitable framework for evaluating the transcriptional response of *Prochlorococcus* under nitrogen starvation.
